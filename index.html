    Chocolate Chip M&M Cookies

    Amazing M&M Cookies. Highschooler approved

    • 1 cup unsalted butter
    • 1 1/2 cups light brown sugar
    • 1/4 granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 3 cups all purpose flour
    • 1 tsp baking soda
    • 1 tsp salt
    • 1 cup chocolate chips
    • 1 cup M&M's, additional M&M's to top to your liking
    1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
    2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugars for 1 minute. Add the eggs and vanilla and mix until combined.
    3. Add the flour, baking soda and salt and stir mix just until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips and M&M’s. Refrigerate dough for 15 to 60 minutes (up to 24 hours) if time allows.
    4. Using a large cookie scoop (about 3 Tablespoons), scoop the cookie dough onto the baking sheet about 2 inches apart. Top with additional m&ms if desired
    5. Bake for 7-10 minutes, or until the edges are barely golden brown. Allow cookies to set on pan for 2 minutes, and then move to a cooling rack. Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days.

    Snickerdoodle Cookies

    • 2 3/4 cup cake flour
    • 2 tsp cream of tartar
    • 1 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1/2 tsp salt
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
    • 1/2 vegetable shortening
    • 1 cup granulated sugar, divided
    • 1 cup light brown sugar, packed
    • 2 large eggs
    • 2 tsp vanilla extact
    • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line a cookie sheet with parchment paper.
    2. In a medium mixing bowl, sift or whisk together the flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt.
    3. In a large bowl, using anf eletric mixer on medium speed or a wooden spoon, beat the butter, shortening, 1/2 of granulated suger, and brown sugar until light and creamy. This will take about 3 minutes with an electric mixer or 5 to 6 minutes by hand.
    4. Add the eggs and vanilla and beat until combined.
    5. Add dry ingredients and beat on a low speed or by hand until dough comes together.
    6. In a shallow bowl mis together the remaining 1/2 cup of suagr and cinnamon for rolling.
    7. Using a medium cookie scoop, scoop the dough into balls, drop each ball into the bowl of cinnamon-sugar, and roll to coat evenly. Place the balls 2 inches apart on the prepared cookie sheets.
    8. Bake for 12 to 13 minutes, until the edges are golden brown
    9. Let the cookies rest on the cookies sheet for 5 minutes before tranfering to a wire cooling rack.

    Red Velvet Cookies

    • 1 box red velvet cake mix
    • 1/3 cup oil
    • 2 large eggs at room temperature
    • 1 cup white chocolate chips
    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper and set aside.
    2. In a large mixing bowl, add the cake mix, oil, and eggs and mix until fully combined.
    3. Add in the white chocolate chips and fold into the dough until well combined.
    4. Scoop two tablespoon sized balls of cookie dough onto the prepared baking sheets, making sure to leave a little room between each one.
    5. Bake for 10 to 12 minutes or until the tops of the cookies are set. Remove from the oven and allow the cookies to cool for 5 to 10 minutes on the baking sheet, then transfer them to a wire rack to finish cooling.

    Double Peanut Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

    • 1/2 cup butter
    • 3/4 cup sugar
    • 1/3 cup creamy peanut butter
    • 1 egg
    • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 1 1/4 cup all- purpose flour
    • 1/2 tsp baking soda
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1 cup milk chocolate chips
    • 1 cup peanut butter chips
    1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
    2. Beat butter, sugar and peanut butter in medium bowl until creamy.
    3. Add egg and vanilla; beat well. Stir together flour, baking soda and salt; add to butter mixture, blending well.
    4. Stir in milk chocolate chips and peanut butter chips. Drop by rounded teaspoons onto ungreased cookie sheets.

    Sugar Cookies

    • 1 cup butter
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 eggs
    • 1 1/2tsp vanilla extract
    • 3/4tsp salt
    • 1 tsp baking powder
    • 1 tsp baking soda
    • 2 1/2 cup allpurpose flour
    1. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper and preheat the oven to 350°F.
    2. In the bowl of a handheld mixer or standing mixer with the paddle attachment, cream together the butter and the sugar until fluffy (around 2 minutes).
    3. Add in the egg and vanilla extract and cream together until light in colour, scraping the sides of the bowl as you go.
    4. Add in the dry ingredients and continue to mix until fully incorporated.
    5. Use a medium sized cookie scoop or a spoon to scoop out 1 ½ tablespoon sized balls of dough onto the baking sheets. If you'd like, you can roll the dough in sugar before baking.
    6. Bake for around 8-11 minutes, or until the edges look set. The middles will look soft. Leave the cookies on the baking tray for 10 minutes, then transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely.

    Vanilla Cupcakes

    • 1 1/2 cup flour
    • 1 1/4 tsp baking powder
    • 1/4 tsp salt
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
    • 1 cup sugar
    • 2 large eggs, room temperature
    • 2 tsp vanilla extract
    • 2/3 cup whole milk, room temperature
    1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Line a 12- cup cupcake pan with liners.
    2. Whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt in a bowl
    3. Beat the butter and sugar in a stand mixer, fitted with the paddle attachment on medium-high speed until fluffy, about 4 minutes.
    4. Beat in eggs one at a time, until combined.
    5. Beat in vanilla
    6. Reduce mixer speed to low, beat in half of the flour mixture, then all the milk, then the rest of the remaining flour mixture until just combined
    7. Divide the batter amoung the muffin cups, filling each cup 3/4 of the way. Bake until toothpick inserted into the center of the cupcake comes out clean, 18 to 20 minutes, rotating the pan halfway through. Let cupcakes cool before removing from the pan.
